# Micro — Single-Ship Control

Micro is how you execute bait, whale avoidance, and circle plays **without moving your whole stack**. Read [Fight in the Circle](fight-on-the-point.md) first — micro is the **how**, circle logic is the **why**.

---

## Read Who Is Targeted

**Practice learning to tell which ship is targeted.**

The easiest tell: **watch which ship is losing health.** Whoever is taking sustained damage is the focus — that's the ship enemies are shooting.

Also watch the **left ship panel** and the field for targeting indicators. You need to know:

- **Am I losing HP?** → I'm the target → move / bait (see below)
- **Is our whale dropping health?** → stack on them or call help
- **Is a low-HP ally melting?** → peel or focus the shooter

If you don't know who's targeted, you can't react in time. Glance at **HP bars every few seconds** in a contested circle.

---

## Move One Ship — Drag From the Left

You can **drag from the targeted ship on the left panel out to a location** to move **that ship alone** — without pulling the rest of your fleets with you.

```
Left panel → click/drag the ship → drop on map location → only that ship moves
```

This is the core micro tool. Use it when:

| Situation | Micro play |
|-----------|------------|
| **You're targeted** | Drag yourself out of the circle — bait chasers off the count |
| **Whale targeting you, we lack numbers** | Drag away; let them chase — [Avoid whale](advanced-tactics.md) |
| **Rest of stack must hold circle** | Only the bait ship moves; teammates stay on point |
| **Reposition one body** | Slide into circle or stack on whale without shifting everyone |

**Don't** move the whole group when only **one ship** needs to kite.

---

## Why Micro Opens Strategy

Without micro, bait and avoid plays are accidental — whole stack follows, count collapses.

With micro:

1. **Bait out** — one ship drags out; chasers leave circle; team holds count ([Advanced Tactics](advanced-tactics.md))
2. **Whale avoid** — targeted ship peels alone; whale wastes time off-point
3. **Stack on whale** — smaller ships drag onto whale position without breaking formation elsewhere
4. **Low-HP peel** — pull a focused ally slightly back while others keep firing in circle

Micro turns "don't chase" and "avoid whale" from **ideas** into **plays you can actually run**.

## Practice Checklist

- [ ] I notice **which ship is losing health** — that's who's targeted
- [ ] I can tell when **my** ship is targeted (HP dropping + left panel)
- [ ] I can drag **one ship** from the left panel to a map spot without moving others
- [ ] When targeted in circle, I drag **out** instead of standing and dying
- [ ] When baiting, I call **"Bait out [vault]"** before I drag
- [ ] After micro kite, I don't drag back in early and re-pull chasers into count

---

## Common Micro Mistakes

**Moving the whole stack** — everyone leaves the circle; count lost. Drag **one ship** only.

**Not watching HP bars** — you eat focus fire unaware. **Losing health = targeted.** Glance at bars often.

**Dragging back in too soon** — chasers re-enter circle with you. Stay out until count flips or coordinator calls.

**Baiting with nobody holding circle** — micro only works if teammates **stay in** the zone.

| Call | When |
|------|------|
| **"I'm targeted"** | You're taking focus — coordinator may call bait |
| **"Bait out [vault]"** | You're dragging one ship out — hold count, team |
| **"Whale on me"** | Whale focus — avoid / kite via micro |
