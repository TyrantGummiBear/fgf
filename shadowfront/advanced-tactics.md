# Advanced Tactics

For players who know the basics. Read [Fight in the Circle](fight-on-the-point.md) first.

---

## Focus Fire — Lowest HP First

Capture flips when **enemy numbers drop**. The fastest way to drop numbers is to **kill ships quickly** — so focus the **lowest HP target in the circle** first.

```
Enemies in circle → pick lowest HP → group focus → number drops → next lowest
        ↓
Repeat until we have count advantage
```

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Call and burn lowest HP **in the circle** | Spread fire across every target |
| Switch to next lowest after a drop | Keep shooting a runner who left the zone |
| Drop numbers fast to flip capture | Pad damage on full-HP tanks |

**Rule:** Lowest HP first **inside the circle**. If they run out — don't chase (see Circle Bait below). Find the next lowest still in zone.

Coordinator call: **"Focus [name]"** or **"Low HP [name]"** — everyone on that target until down.

## Enemy Whales — Focus With Numbers, Avoid Without

Enemy whales hit hard. **How we play them depends on whether we have the count.**

### We have numbers → Group focus

When we **outnumber them in the circle** and an enemy whale enters the fray — **everyone burn it together**.

- Whales still **count as one body** — dropping them removes their number fast
- We have enough bodies left to absorb their damage while we focus
- Burn the whale, then back to **lowest HP** among what's left

**Call:** **"Focus whale [name]"** — all fire until down or forced out.

### We don't have numbers → Avoid, don't feed

When we're **outnumbered or even** in the circle — **do not stand and trade with the whale**. You'll die, count won't flip, and they keep capturing.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Avoid engaging the whale directly | Stand in circle and get deleted |
| **Move when they target you** — kite, reposition | Hold still as their focus target |
| If they **chase you out** — keep going (bait) | Turn back into the circle with chasers on your tail |
| Peel to **lowest HP non-whale** targets if safe | Dump all fire into whale while losing count |

**Goal when avoiding:** Lower the whale's value. A whale **chasing one ship outside the circle** is wasted — they're off the count and not flipping capture. **Drag that one ship out** via micro ([Micro](micro.md)) — don't move your whole stack.

1. Whale targets you → call **"Bait out [vault]"** or **"Whale on me"**
2. **Drag your ship out of the circle** from the left panel
3. Teammates **stay in** and work lowest HP targets or hold count
4. If whale doesn't chase, **don't drag into them** — skirt, stay alive, wait for stack

**Call:** **"Avoid whale"** — nobody trades with it; kite if targeted.

## Our Whales — Stack on Top

Our big fleets are priority targets. **Stack on top of our whales** inside the circle so enemies can't isolate them.

- Bodies overlapping / stacked = harder to single-target our whale
- Enemy has to dig through the pile or waste focus on smaller targets
- Whale holds count; smaller ships **anchor around them**, not spread in a line outside

### How to stack on our whale
1. Move into the **same circle space** as our whale when coordinator calls stack
2. Stay in circle — stacking outside the zone doesn't help
3. If whale is focused, **lowest-HP enemies first** — drop their numbers while our whale soaks

**Call:** **"Stack on [name]"** — form up on our whale in the circle.

## Circle Bait — Don't Chase / Bait Them Out

Capture is about **who leaves the circle**. Bait is the intentional version of the chase trap.

### Don't chase out

**If an enemy runs out of the circle, do not follow them.**

They left the count. Hold the circle and find the next target **still inside**.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Let runners go | Chase low-HP out of zone |
| Re-focus lowest HP **in circle** | Abandon count for one kill |

### Bait them out (when you're the target)

**If you're a single ship being focused and enemies chase you — kite them out of the circle.**

Use **single-ship micro** — drag your ship from the left panel to a location outside the circle so only you move. See [Micro](micro.md).

```
Enemies chase you OUT → they lose count → teammates hold / flip inside
```

1. Call **"Bait out [vault]"**
2. **Drag your ship out** from the left panel — don't move the whole stack
3. Teammates **stay in** and win the number fight

**Your job as bait:** Pull attention outside. Count win > solo survival.

## Target Priority (Full Order)

When fighting in a contested circle:

1. **Lowest HP enemy in the circle** (default — when we have or are fighting for count)
2. **Enemy whale in the circle** — **group focus only if we have numbers**
3. **Whale targeting you, we lack numbers** — **avoid, move, bait out** if they chase
4. **Runners who left the circle** — ignore (don't chase)
5. **Tower** — when big incoming or count allows ([Fight in the Circle](fight-on-the-point.md))

After any drop, **re-scan for new lowest HP in circle**.

```
Do we have number advantage in circle?
├── YES + whale in fray → Focus whale
├── NO  + whale in fray → Avoid; kite if targeted
└── Either way → lowest HP first when not on whale focus
```

## Coordinator Calls

| Call | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **"Focus [name]"** / **"Low HP [name]"** | Burn this target first |
| **"Focus whale [name]"** | All fire on enemy whale — **we have numbers** |
| **"Avoid whale"** | Don't trade — kite if targeted, bait if they chase |
| **"Stack on [name]"** | Group on our whale in circle |
| **"Bait out [vault]"** | Kite — rest hold count |
| **"Don't chase"** | Runner left zone — stay in circle |

## Common Advanced Mistakes

**Spread fire** — everyone on a different target; nobody drops. Focus lowest HP.

**Feeding the whale outnumbered** — standing in circle trading while losing count. Avoid and kite.

**Ignoring enemy whale when we HAVE numbers** — it keeps deleting our stack. Group focus.

**Chasing a whale out of circle** — same as any runner; don't follow. Let them leave if we're avoiding.

**Our whale alone in circle** — easy pick. Stack on them.

**Whole squad chases bait** — one chaser max on bait plays; rest hold count.
