# Fight in the Circle

ShadowFront plays like **Call of Duty Control**. Each vault has a **capture circle** around it. Fleets inside that circle count toward capture — whoever has **the most numbers in the circle wins**, not the strongest ships.

---

## How Capture Works

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Capture zone** | Circle around the vault / tower |
| **What counts** | Fleets **inside the circle** contribute to capture |
| **Who wins** | Side with **more fleets in the circle** — numbers, not power |
| **Outside the circle** | Fights out here do **not** help capture |

If we're trading damage outside the circle while the enemy stacks inside it, **they capture**. Every time.

---

## The Problem We Keep Seeing

Players fight **outside the circle** or chase targets that don't change capture.

- Attacking from range while enemy holds the circle
- Chasing a low-HP fleet out of the zone
- Farming damage on pass-through traffic
- Bringing one strong fleet instead of stacking numbers

**None of that flips capture.** They keep the number advantage in the circle.

---

## Fight Priority (Default)

When a vault is being contested, target in this order:

### 1. Remove enemy players in the circle (if they're capturing)

If the enemy has more numbers in the circle — **kill or force out fleets inside the circle first**. Every ship removed shifts the count.

**Focus lowest HP in the circle first** — fastest kills = fastest number drop = faster capture. Don't spread damage. See [Advanced Tactics](advanced-tactics.md) for focus fire and whale stacking.

### 2. Clear enough enemies to take the guardian

After capture, a **Vault Guardian** blocks re-capture until it's killed — see [Vaults & Guardians](vaults.md). Remove enough enemy players in the circle that **when the guardian goes down, we win the number fight** and hold the zone.

Don't burn the guardian while enemies still outnumber us inside — they'll recapture the moment it falls.

### 3. Get our fleets into the circle

After kills or as kills happen, **step in**. Empty kills outside the zone don't capture. Someone has to be in the circle to convert the fight.

---

## Exceptions (When Priority Changes)

### Big incoming → Guardian first

If you're taking **heavy incoming** and the **Vault Guardian** is the main threat, **guardian takes priority** over chasing individual players. Drop the guardian, then re-establish numbers in the circle.

Coordinator may call **"Guardian first [vault]"** or **"Tower first [vault]"** when this applies.

### Can't win capture → Deny bonus only

If we **cannot win the number fight** in time, stop trying to flip. Goal becomes **preventing bonus points** — see [Bonus Points](bonus-points.md).

Break their countdown late enough that they can't recover. Player removal still matters, but we are **not** committing to holding the circle.

Coordinator calls **"Deny [vault]"** — different goal, different stop point.

---

## Default Play

```
Coordinator calls a vault
        ↓
Move into the capture circle
        ↓
Stack numbers — more fleets in circle than them
        ↓
Remove enemy players IN the circle (or clear for tower)
        ↓
Hold number advantage until capture completes
```

---

## Kill Chasing Is a Trap

The #1 way we lose vaults:

> Enemy runs or baits us **outside the circle** → we chase → they keep more numbers inside → capture continues.

**Rule:** If an enemy **leaves the circle**, don't follow — hold the count. Remove enemies **still in the zone** or let runners go.

**Advanced:** If *you're* the single ship being chased, kiting **out** of the circle is correct — pull them off the count so teammates win inside. See [Advanced Tactics — Circle Bait](advanced-tactics.md).

---

## Trading in the Circle

Sometimes losing your fleet inside the circle is correct — you removed an enemy, and a teammate fills the spot with better numbers.

- You fight **inside the circle**
- Teammate stays in to keep the count
- You respawn and **return to the same vault** unless rotation is called

---

## Self-Check (Mid-Match)

Every 30 seconds:

1. Am I inside the capture circle?
2. Who has more numbers in the circle right now?
3. Am I targeting enemies **in the circle** or farming outside it?
4. Should I be on players, guardian, or deny? (see priority above)

---

## Summary

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Fight inside the capture circle | Fight outside it |
| Stack numbers, not just big ships | Assume one whale wins the point |
| Remove enemies in the circle first | Chase kills out of zone |
| Focus lowest HP in circle | Spread fire across targets |
| Group focus enemy whales in circle | Trade with whale while outnumbered |
| Stack on our whales in circle | Leave whale isolated |
| Take guardian when count allows | Burn guardian while outnumbered |
| Switch to deny when we can't win | Force a flip we can't hold |

**Capture is a headcount game inside the circle. Play for numbers.**
